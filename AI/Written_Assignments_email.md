Technical details

 

The objective of the written assignments is to provide students with suitable background training on the underlying numerical methods and techniques which are necessary to complete the research project.

 

The word count expectation for each written assignment is within the range of 3,000 to 5,000 words, with the maximum word limit set at 5,000 words. This is including figure captions and references. Reports that are significantly outside the maximum word limit will have marks deducted from their Quality of Work marking, as described in the Course Handbook.

 

Students are required to write the assignments in the LaTeX typesetting language using a research journal template, so as to resemble an article submitted for publication. This is part of their training for communicating their research in the scientific literature and in conference proceedings.

Submission

 

The submission deadline for the two written assignments is 12:00 noon on Friday 13th March 2026 and candidates who fail to meet this deadline without advance permission from the Course Director will normally get no credit for this part of the assessment.

 

You can submit the assignments in any order through the following folders:

 

Submission 2025-26 - Assignment 1
Submission 2025-26 - Assignment 2
 

For each assignment you have to submit:

Your written report which complies with the template, in PDF format
Any source-code/scripts that you have written in the course of your project
 

You must use your Blind Candidate Number on your written assignments, as well as any other additional file you submit; these must not contain any identifying information such as your name or student number. The BCN list is attached (this is the same as for your written examinations).

 

You will also need to submit an anti-plagiarism declaration form for each of your written assignments, to be completed with your full name (not your BGN) and uploaded to the dedicated folder:

Submission 2025-26 - declarations
 

The written assignments are marked by suitable assessors, but the marks are subject to moderation by the Examiners of the course. The written assignments will be made available to the research project dissertation examiners.

 

IMPORTANT – additional step

Students must also share their Overleaf projects (click on ‘Share’ on the top-right of the screen while logged into Overleaf and on your project page) with srs53@cam.ac.uk so that we can verify wordcount compliance. You must do this by the same deadline of 13th March 2026 at 12:00 noon.

 

Extensions

Students who feel they are struggling to meet the submission deadline, or have been negatively affected by a medical issue or other unforeseen circumstances, may request an extension to the submission deadline by emailing admin@csc.cam.ac.uk . Extensions are granted on a case-by-case basis by the Course Director, up to a maximum of one week where possible. For details of how to request an extension, please see ‘Coursework Extension’ on page 21 of the Course Handbook.

 

Anti-plagiarism measures

 

In response to growing concerns over plagiarism in all University courses, each piece of submitted work must be accompanied by a standard cover sheet, including a signed declaration (attached) to the effect that the work is the student’s own unaided effort and meets the University’s guidelines and regulations on plagiarism.

 

Students are required to provide the input files, analysis procedures, and plotting scripts used in generating the results presented in their written assignments. In cases where students have personally written source code for their projects, it is crucial to submit this alongside the other data. While it is common to utilize and adapt existing research software, it is essential to clearly indicate your contributions and changes. The university's plagiarism regulations also extend to the submitted source code. Assessors may refer to the submitted code and scripts to evaluate your work.

 

The written assignments (and source-code) must not contain any personal identifying information, except for the student’s Blind Candidate Number, so that course staff can make use of TurnItIn and other plagiarism detection tools on the work. The University policy on TurnItIn can be found here.
